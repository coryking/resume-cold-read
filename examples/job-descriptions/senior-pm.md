## Description

Meridian AI's Developer Experience product area is responsible for every surface a working engineer touches: the IDE extension, the PR review workflow, the CLI, the configuration experience, and the feedback loops that make Meridian Studio feel like it understands your codebase rather than just analyzing it. This is the product area with the highest daily active usage, the sharpest user feedback, and the most direct line between your decisions and whether engineers actually love the tool.

We're looking for a Senior PM who thinks like an engineer, communicates like a product leader, and is genuinely curious about how developers work.

### What You'll Do

- Own the roadmap for developer-facing product surfaces: IDE integrations, CLI, PR workflow, and onboarding
- Drive customer discovery with engineering teams at Meridian's customers — not just surveys, but shadowing sessions, prototype tests, and ongoing relationships with power users
- Define and own success metrics for your product area; build dashboards, run experiments, and use data to make hard prioritization calls
- Partner with engineering leads on technical scoping, write tight PRDs, and ruthlessly cut scope to ship things that work
- Collaborate with the AI Features team to surface model capabilities in ways developers actually trust and use
- Work cross-functionally with Design, Data Science, and GTM to align on positioning and rollout
- Present strategy and results to leadership; represent the customer in architectural discussions

### What We're Looking For

- 5+ years of product management experience, with at least 2 years owning a product area with measurable business or user outcomes
- Technical background: you've written production code, done a CS degree, or can credibly read a PR and understand what it changes
- Track record of shipping products to B2B SaaS customers, including navigating enterprise procurement and feedback cycles
- Strong written communication — you can write a one-pager that gets engineers aligned and a slide that gets executives bought in
- Comfort with ambiguity: you don't need a fully-defined brief to start moving
- Data fluency: you've built dashboards, run A/B tests, and know the difference between a vanity metric and a leading indicator

### Nice to Have

- Experience building products used by software engineers or developer tool practitioners
- Familiarity with code review systems, CI/CD pipelines, or static analysis tooling as a user or builder
- Experience working with AI/ML-powered product features — prompting, evaluation, latency/cost tradeoffs
- Hands-on experience with qualitative research methods: user interviews, contextual inquiry, usability testing

### Why This Role

Developer Experience PMs have a rare advantage: their users are technical, opinionated, and will tell you exactly what's wrong. That's a gift, not a burden. This role has high visibility inside Meridian (DX metrics drive retention), strong cross-functional relationships, and a product area where incremental improvements produce measurable daily-active-usage gains.

Meridian offers competitive base compensation, meaningful equity, fully-covered medical/dental/vision, 401k with 4% match, $2,000/year learning budget, and 20 days PTO plus company holidays.
