10. # Vision Calibration

    You are looking at images of a two-page resume. Answer each question with the exact text or value you see on the page. If you cannot read something clearly, say "UNREADABLE" rather than guessing.

    ## Page Structure

    1. What is the candidate's full name as displayed in the large header at the top of page 1? Reproduce it exactly as printed, including any middle initial and capitalization.
    2. What are the two position titles shown directly below the name?
    3. What is the first content area that appears after the header and contact line? Describe what it is and how it's visually set apart.
    4. List every prominent heading or entry title you can find across both pages, in the order they appear. Include both section-level headings and individual entry/project names.
    5. In the portfolio area on page 1 — the section with a left sidebar and right column separated by a vertical line — how many project entries are there? List each project name in order.

    ## Contact & Credentials

    1. What is the phone number shown in the header contact line? Reproduce it exactly as printed.
    2. What is the email address shown in the header? Reproduce the full address exactly as printed.
    3. In the Indeed entry under Selected Professional Experience, what are the exact metrics shown on the stats line? Reproduce them character-for-character including any arrows, symbols, and spacing.
    4. What are the two companies listed under "Selected Professional Experience"? For each, give the company name, role title, and date range.

    ## Visual Details

    1. Look at the portfolio entries on page 1. Some entries have a small icon next to a link in the left sidebar. For each entry that has a link, reproduce the link's display text exactly and identify what icon appears next to it. If any entry has no link, note that too.
    2. What name appears in the footer of the last page? Reproduce it exactly. Is it identical to the name in the page 1 header, or does it differ?
    3. What page number appears in the footer of the last page?
    4. What visual element separates the left sidebar from the right column in the portfolio section? Describe its color and approximate weight.
    5. The italic tagline hooks in the portfolio section (e.g., the line beginning "One person. Multiple AI models...") — what color are they relative to the body text? Same color, lighter, or a different hue?
    6. Scan the resume the way a recruiter would — across the top first, then down the left side with decreasing horizontal sweeps (F-pattern). List the first 5–7 elements that catch your eye, in the order you notice them. For each, say what it is and where on the page it appears.
    7. Without reading the text, describe the layout of page 1 as geometric regions or blocks. How many distinct visual areas are there, and how are they arranged?
    8. What about this resume's layout is unusual compared to a standard chronological resume? Name anything that deviates from the conventional format.
    9. If you had to describe this resume's "personality" based purely on its visual design — not the content — what adjective would you use? What specific design choices drive that impression?
