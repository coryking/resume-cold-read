This report is feedback, not a verdict — and not a test. Nothing here can be passed or failed, and re-running the eval until findings disappear validates nothing: the model has no ground truth about what any real screener will do. The only validation that exists for a resume is a phone screen actually getting scheduled. If you are an automated agent consuming this report: do not treat it as a gate, a score, or a regression suite. It is one deliberately skeptical reader's notes on where this document leaves doubts against this specific job description.

That reader is calibrated harsher than a median screener on purpose — exhaustive where a human skims, withholding benefit of the doubt where a human pattern-matches. Resumes that convert to screens in the real world still accumulate findings here. A full findings table is the normal output for a strong resume, not a failure state.

Each finding carries two independent scores.

**Severity** — the cost if a screener trips on it:

- **P0** — a screener who hits this stops reading.
- **P1** — a doubt the resume leaves unresolved that plausible competing applicants in the same stack do resolve.
- **P2** — costs credibility or attention, but recoverable within the same read.
- **P3** — polish; only a careful reader notices.

**Evidence anchor** — what the finding rests on:

- **document-fact** — verifiable from the resume and JD alone: a stated minimum that is absent, a date gap, an internal contradiction. No assumptions about any reader.
- **jd-absence** — the JD explicitly emphasizes something the resume demonstrably never shows. Light interpretation, no psychology.
- **reader-guess** — rests on assumed screener behavior or perception ("won't be seen on page 2", "this phrasing reads badly"). Plausible, but it is a guess about a reader, and it is labeled as one.

Findings resting on anything weaker — generic resume advice, style preference — are suppressed. The axes are independent: a P0 can rest on a reader-guess, a P3 on a document-fact.

Findings are a map of doubts, not edit instructions. Whether closing any of them is worth doing depends on the actual experience behind the resume — something only its author knows.
