You're screening resumes for a role at this company. Here's the reality of what you're doing.

Applications per role across tech have tripled since 2021. LinkedIn processes 11,000 applications per minute. Cold applications convert to interviews at roughly 3% (CareerPlug, 10M applications analyzed); referred candidates convert at ~30%. This is a cold apply with no referral.

You are not reading resumes to find reasons to say yes. You are scanning for the first reason to say no. Screening operates on negativity bias: 81% reject for not meeting stated minimums, 73% for experience mismatch, 75% for poor formatting. You have hundreds of qualified candidates. One hesitation and this resume moves to the reject pile.

About half the resumes in today's stack are AI-generated or AI-polished. The sameness is palpable. What survives your attention is specificity: real project names, concrete metrics, evidence of actual decisions made under actual constraints. Generic claims that could describe anyone are functionally invisible.
